Our Story

Built by someone who has sat
on both sides of every hire.

HireSignalIQ was not built in a vacuum. It was built by Gary Bresien — a senior executive search practitioner with 25 years of experience running searches across tech firms, AI startups, cybersecurity organizations, and executive leadership roles. Every feature, every prompt, every output section reflects something learned from an actual search, an actual candidate, or an actual hiring manager conversation.

That provenance matters. Every competitor in this space was built by engineers. HireSignalIQ was built by someone who has made the calls, read the resumes, run the intake sessions, and placed the candidates. The AI thinks like a headhunter because a headhunter built it.

His most recent focus has been on AI-centric tech startups, where speed of hire, quality of fit, and search precision are mission-critical.

The founding insight

The question that started everything.

In the years leading up to HireSignalIQ's founding, Gary was deeply embedded in the AI tech startup ecosystem — not just as an observer, but as an active participant. He was recruiting for AI-centric companies, participating in AI community events and meetups as part of sponsoring organizations, and at times speaking within that community. He was watching clients ship products that were genuinely changing the pace of entire industries.

"If my clients can move this fast with AI — why am I still building search briefs the same way I did ten years ago?"

That question didn't come from a whiteboard session or a business plan. It came from inside the work itself — from being close enough to the technology to recognize that the same advances reshaping his clients' products could reshape his own practice. The more Gary explored that question, the more he realized the opportunity wasn't just to automate repetitive tasks. It was to encode genuine recruiting expertise into an AI engine and make that expertise available to everyone who needs it — on both sides of the hire.

HireSignalIQ is the answer to that question.

Product philosophy

Four principles that drive every decision.

HireSignalIQ is not a feature list. It is a set of convictions about what recruiting intelligence should look like — and what it should never become.

🎯
Quality over volume
HireSignalIQ does not auto-apply on anyone's behalf. The platform was built on the conviction that one targeted, well-prepared application is worth more than a hundred spray-and-pray submissions — and the market is proving that right in 2026 as recruiters actively filter out mass-applied candidates.
⚡
Elevation, not just automation
The goal is not to replace the work. It is to elevate the output. A recruiter using HireSignalIQ doesn't just save four hours — they start every search with a better brief than most would produce manually. A job seeker doesn't just save time — they apply with the confidence of someone who has a real recruiter in their corner.
🔄
Both sides of the hire
No competitor serves recruiters and job seekers from the same platform. HireSignalIQ does — because the expertise that makes a great recruiter also makes a great job search. The same intelligence engine powers both products, and users on each side benefit from the knowledge built for the other.
📄
You own your outputs
Every deliverable arrives as an editable Google Doc. No locked PDFs. No proprietary dashboard you have to learn. No data held hostage behind a subscription. Your outputs are yours — ready to edit, share, download, or hand off the moment they arrive.
What you get

Every section. Every output. No surprises.

HireSignalIQ delivers complete, structured intelligence — not a summary, not a snippet. Here is exactly what arrives in your inbox within minutes of submitting your inputs.

The recruiter product delivers three Google Documents — a Search Brief, a Screening & Interview Kit, and a rewritten Job Description — plus a summary email with all document links and a unique Search ID for future updates.

Google Doc 1 Search Brief
⌄

The Search Brief is the operational foundation of every search. It captures what the job description doesn't say, calibrates targets against real candidate examples, and gives a recruiter everything needed to source, pitch, and screen — without starting from scratch.

  • Inputs Summary
    Confirms which inputs were provided and how the brief was calibrated — JD only, with candidate profiles, or with intake notes.
  • Role Summary
    A concise synthesis of the role — what it is, what success looks like, and how it fits within the organization.
  • What the Hiring Manager Actually Wants
    Extracted from intake notes — the unstated preferences, real priorities, and cultural signals that never make it into the job description.
  • Must-Have Competencies
    The non-negotiable skills, experiences, and attributes. Used to qualify candidates at first contact.
  • Nice-to-Have Competencies
    Differentiating factors that strengthen a candidate's fit without being disqualifying if absent.
  • Red Flags to Screen For
    Specific warning signs — background gaps, role mismatches, experience patterns — that indicate a candidate is unlikely to succeed.
  • HireSignal Profile Analysis
    When candidate profiles are submitted, the AI identifies patterns across their backgrounds and sharpens every output section accordingly.
  • Target Companies
    A curated list of companies most likely to employ the right candidate — calibrated to the role requirements and candidate profile patterns.
  • Adjacent Talent Pools
    Non-obvious sourcing channels — adjacent industries, titles, company types — that expand the search without diluting quality.
  • Boolean Search Strings
    Three ready-to-use Boolean strings — core title and skills, adjacent titles, and company-targeted — formatted for LinkedIn Recruiter.
  • LinkedIn Recruiter Search Recipe
    Three fully specified LinkedIn Recruiter searches — title filters, seniority settings, company targets, keyword Booleans, and exclusions — ready to copy directly.
  • InMail & Outreach Intelligence
    What to lead with, what will attract this candidate, what will repel them, a suggested opening line, and what not to say.
  • Compensation Intelligence
    Market range for base, bonus, and equity where available — with positioning guidance for how to present the package.
  • Search Timeline Recommendation
    A phased week-by-week plan from initial sourcing through offer — calibrated to the complexity of the search.
  • Suggested Next Steps
    Specific, prioritized actions for the recruiter to take in the next 48–72 hours to move the search forward.
Google Doc 2 Screening & Interview Kit
⌄

The Screening & Interview Kit gives any team member a consistent, role-specific framework for evaluating candidates. No more improvised screening calls.

  • Candidate Scorecard
    A weighted scoring framework with 4–5 evaluation criteria, each assigned a percentage weight that reflects the true priorities of the role.
  • Role-Specific Screening Questions
    5–7 questions calibrated to the specific role, level, and competencies — not generic interview questions.
  • Strong & Weak Answer Guides
    For each question, a description of what a strong answer looks like and what a weak answer looks like.
  • 3-Point Scoring Rubric
    A simple Strong / Acceptable / Weak rubric for every question — makes post-interview debriefs faster and candidate comparisons more objective.
Google Doc 3 Rewritten Job Description
⌄

Produced when requested at intake. Choose internal format, confidential third-party format, or both.

  • Internal Version
    An optimized job description for use on the company's own careers page — company name included, structured for clarity and candidate appeal.
  • Confidential Third-Party Version
    A version that protects the client's identity — company name and identifying details removed — for use by recruiters posting on behalf of clients.

Note: The JD Rewrite output is currently being enhanced to deliver more substantial, fully restructured job descriptions.

Included Update Search Criteria
⌄

  • Search ID Lookup

The job seeker product delivers one comprehensive Google Document — your Application Brief — plus a personal Google Sheets application tracker that grows with every brief you generate.

Google Doc Application Brief
⌄

The Application Brief replaces hours of manual tailoring with a complete, role-specific intelligence package. The moment you open it, the relief is immediate: this application is not a shot in the dark.

  • Role & Company Header
    The title and company name at the top of the brief — your anchor for every section that follows.
  • HireSignal Score
    Your match percentage for this specific role — broken down into Skills, Experience, Industry, and Intangibles. Know before you apply whether this role is worth your time.
  • ATS Keyword Analysis
    Every keyword the ATS is scanning for — marked as covered, partially covered, or missing from your current resume.
  • Tailored Resume Bullets
    Role-specific resume bullets written in your voice — calibrated to the language and priorities of this job posting, not copied from a generic library.
  • Tailored Professional Summary
    A rewritten professional summary that leads with what this hiring manager is looking for — not a generic paragraph about your career.
  • Cover Letter
    A three-paragraph cover letter that sounds like you — specific to the role, credible in tone, and recruiter-readable.
  • LinkedIn Optimization
    Headline rewrites, About section guidance, skills to feature, and a connection strategy — all specific to this role.
  • Networking & Application Strategy
    A targeted plan for who to reach out to, what to say, and how to approach the search strategically.
  • Application Recommendation
    A direct recommendation — apply now, apply with these caveats, or reconsider — with specific guidance on how to position yourself.
Google Sheet Personal Application Tracker
⌄

Every brief you generate automatically populates your personal Application Tracker — a Google Sheet that lives in your own Google Drive and grows with your search.

  • Auto-Populated on Every Submission
    Each brief adds a new row — role title, company, date, HireSignal Score, and a direct link to your Application Brief Google Doc.
  • Application Status Tracking
    A dropdown for each role — Applied, Not Applied, Interviewing, Rejected — so you always know where each opportunity stands.
  • Your Data, Your Drive
    The tracker lives in your personal Google Drive — not inside HireSignalIQ. You own it completely.
The principle behind the product

Helping people has always been the job.

Long before HireSignalIQ existed, Gary was spending hours on the phone with candidates he had no open role for. No placement fee at the end. No business reason to take the call. Just someone navigating a difficult job search who needed a real recruiter's perspective — and Gary giving it to them.

These weren't rare exceptions. They were a consistent thread across his career. Laid-off executives. Career changers. First-time job seekers trying to understand how the process actually works from the other side. Gary would walk through LinkedIn strategies, networking approaches, how to pursue conversations with intention, how to work smarter rather than harder. Candidates were consistently grateful — and consistently puzzled that he wasn't charging for it.

"They would always ask why I wasn't charging for this. The honest answer was that helping people navigate something complex and come out the other side with a real advantage — that's just what I do."

— Gary Bresien, Founder, HireSignalIQ

HireSignalIQ is, in part, the answer to that question. The same expertise Gary was giving away for free in phone calls is now inside every output — available to any recruiter or job seeker who needs it, at any hour, for any role, at a price that reflects what it should have cost all along.
